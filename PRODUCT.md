# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Job seekers actively managing a career search — typically mid-career professionals applying to multiple roles simultaneously. They use Trackr during focused work sessions: logging applications, prepping for interviews, reviewing AI coaching output, and tracking how many hours they've invested. They're self-directed and goal-oriented; they chose this tool over a spreadsheet because they want structure and intelligence, not just storage.

## Product Purpose

Trackr is a job application management platform that gives job seekers a command centre for their search. It combines a Kanban pipeline (Board), time tracking (Engage widget + Time Report), AI coaching tools (Interview Coach, Salary Intel, Cover Letter, Offer Simulator), stats, and a CV hub in one dark, data-dense interface. Success looks like a user who feels in control of their search and can see, at a glance, exactly where they stand and what to do next.

## Brand Personality

Calm, structured, intelligent. Trackr is the quiet expert — it holds all the complexity without making the user feel overwhelmed. It doesn't celebrate loudly or guilt-trip; it just shows the data and lets the user decide. Think of it as the difference between a Bloomberg terminal and a fitness app leaderboard.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard** — the sidebar + card grid that every B2B tool ships. Trackr should feel purpose-built for job hunting, not like a white-label admin template with a new logo.
- **Bright, colorful, gamified** — no streak counters, no confetti, no Duolingo-style encouragement. This is a serious tool for people in a stressful life situation. Delight comes from clarity and precision, not animation and badges.
- **LinkedIn / recruitment portal energy** — corporate blue, approachable-but-bland, optimized for mass-market trust. Trackr serves power users who want depth.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over noise** — every element on screen is either data the user needs or an action they can take. Decorative chrome is cut. Labels, borders, and section headers should encode something true about the content hierarchy, not decorate.
2. **Intelligence made visible** — AI features should feel like a quiet expert in the corner, not a chatbot widget. Surface insights inline, in the flow, with the same visual weight as other data.
3. **Structure is trust** — consistent spatial rhythm, predictable navigation, and stable layouts let users scan without thinking. Don't surprise people who are already stressed.
4. **Dark glass precision** — the aesthetic is a deliberate choice: near-black surfaces, electric cyan accents, zero border-radius on rectangles, Geist + Geist Mono typography. This is not an edgy skin; it's the brand. Never drift toward softness (rounded corners, warm backgrounds, pastel accents).
5. **Density without clutter** — job searches involve a lot of data. Embrace information density but enforce hierarchy: large numbers, small labels, tight spacing, clear grouping. The eye should always know where to land first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the floor. Key considerations: the dark palette must maintain ≥4.5:1 contrast for all body text (the most common failure point at these light-on-dark ratios); every status color must have a non-color indicator (shape, label, or icon) for color-blind users; all interactive elements need visible focus rings; `prefers-reduced-motion` must be respected on any animation.
